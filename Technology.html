Computer Basics: Hardware

What is a Computer?

A computer is an electrical device that processes data and tasks based on instructions. The physical parts of a computer consists of a CPU (central processing unit), GPU (graphics processing unit), keyboard and mouse, RAM (random access memory), a motherboard, a power supply and a HDD (hard drive).

What is a CPU?

A central processing unit is the core of a computer. It is the part of the computer that thinks and makes decisions. Without a CPU, a computer will not work. Imagine your baking a cake. The recipe book tells you what to do. You (CPU) read the instruction, mix the ingredients and put the cake in the oven. The oven helps cook the cake, but you are the one telling the oven how long it should be on for and what temperature it should be.

What is RAM?

Random access memory is the computer's short term memory. It stores information that the computer is actively using so that it can quickly access it when needed. Think of RAM like your school desk. When you're working, you put your books and pencil case on the desk to use them easily. When your lesson is done, you put everything in your bag. The bigger your desk is the more books and pens you can put on it and the less time you have to spend looking in your bag.

What is HDD?

A hard disk drive is the computers long term memory. It stores all your files, games and apps until you delete them manually. Think of a HDD like a library. Inside a library there are a lot of books that are well organised so that it’s easy to find the book you’re looking for. When you need a book, you go to the shelf and pick it. Inside a HDD, there is a spinning magnetic disk where the data is stored.

What is a GPU?

A graphics processing unit is a part of a computer that helps create images, videos and animations. It makes games look very realistic and also helps editors with video editing. The better the GPU, the faster games will run.

What is a Motherboard?

A motherboard is the main circuit board on a computer. It connects all the main components so they can communicate with each other. Without a motherboard, the computer won’t be able to function because all the computer parts will not be able to communicate with each other. Think of a motherboard like a train station. Different trains will arrive at the station. The track connects everything together. To prevent trains from colliding with each other, they have to communicate. Furthermore, if a train track breaks, no train will be able to leave the train station.

What is a power supply?

A power supply is the heart of the computer. The computer will not be able to turn on without a power supply. It takes electricity from a socket and transfers it to the computer. Think of it like a phone charger. Your phone needs electricity to charge. Without a charger, your phone will eventually die until you plug your phone on charge.

Computer Basics: Software

What is Software

Software is the set of programs and instructions that tell a computer what to do. Without software, a computer would just be a box of hardware that doesn’t know how to work. There are different types of software, such as operating systems, applications, and games.

What is an Operating System

An operating system (OS) is the main software that manages the computer and helps it run smoothly. It controls how different parts of the computer talk to each other and lets you use apps. Think of an OS like the manager of McDonalds. The manager makes sure that the chefs, waiters, and cashiers work together so that McDonalds runs properly.

What are applications?

Applications or apps are programs on a computer. Some apps help you write documents, play games, edit videos or browse the internet. Think of apps like different tools in a toolbox. A hammer helps you build, a screwdriver helps you tighten screws and a wrench helps you fix things. Each app has its own job.

What is a Web Browser?

A web browser is a type of application that lets you browse the internet. Popular web browsers include Google Chrome, Firefox and Microsoft Edge. Think of a web browser like a car that takes you on a journey through the internet. Just like a car needs roads, a browser needs websites to travel to (and internet).

Internet Basics

What is the Internet?

The internet is a giant network that connects computers and devices all around the world. It allows people to share information, send messages, and visit websites. Think of the internet like a huge spider web that connects cities, schools, and homes, allowing people to communicate and find information on the go.

How does the Internet Work?

The internet works by sending small packets of data between computers through cables, Wi-Fi, and satellites. Websites, videos, and messages also travel across this network to reach your computer. Think of it like a postal system, where letters (data) travel through different post offices (servers) before reaching the right address (your device).
